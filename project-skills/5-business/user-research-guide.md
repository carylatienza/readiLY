# User Research Guide (Interviews & Validation)

> **How to use:** Run problem interviews BEFORE building, solution tests once you have a prototype. 5-8 interviews per segment is usually enough to see patterns. The cardinal rule: **ask about their life, not your idea** — people are polite and will say they'd "definitely use it." Past behavior is evidence; compliments are not.

## 1. Research plan (fill in before talking to anyone)

- **What we need to learn:** [the 1-3 riskiest assumptions — e.g. "teachers actually spend 15+ min per oral assessment"]
- **Who to talk to:** [segment + how many — e.g. 6 Grade 1-3 public school teachers]
- **How we'll recruit:** [personal network, school visits, teacher Facebook groups, referrals from each interview]
- **Success looks like:** [what evidence would confirm/kill each assumption]

## 2. Problem interview script (~30 min)

> Record (with permission) or take verbatim notes. Talk 20% of the time, max.

**Warm-up (5 min)**
- Tell me about your role and a typical day.
- [Context question specific to the problem area]

**Dig into the problem (15 min)**
- Walk me through the *last time* you [did the task — e.g. assessed a student's reading]. Step by step, what did you do?
- What's the hardest/most annoying part of that? Why?
- How often does that happen? How much time does it take?
- What have you tried to make it better? What happened? *(If they've tried nothing, the pain may not be real)*
- If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing about this, what would it be?

**Wrap (5 min)**
- Who else struggles with this? Could you introduce me? *(recruiting flywheel)*
- Anything I should have asked but didn't?

**Never ask:** "Would you use an app that…?" / "Do you like this idea?" / any question about the future or hypotheticals.

## 3. Solution test script (once a prototype exists)

- Give a task, not a tour: "Here's the app — try assessing this sample reading." Then be silent.
- Note every hesitation, wrong tap, and "how do I…?" — those are the findings.
- Afterward: "Would you use this next week in your class? What would stop you?" — then the real test: **ask for a commitment** (time for a pilot, a letter of intent, a pre-order, an intro to their principal). Commitment = validation; "looks great!" = nothing.

## 6. Lightweight survey (optional, for quantifying after interviews)

- Only survey AFTER interviews tell you which questions matter. Keep to ≤10 questions, ≤3 minutes.
- Prefer behavior questions ("How many times last week did you…") over opinion scales.
- Always end with: "Can we contact you for a follow-up? [email]" — that's your pilot waitlist.
