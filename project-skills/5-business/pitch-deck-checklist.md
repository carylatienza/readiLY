# Pitch Deck Checklist

> **How to use:** Run through this when building or revising a deck for a competition, investor, or partner. The deck's job is to earn the *next conversation*, not to explain everything. Rule of thumb: 10-12 slides, one idea per slide, headline states the takeaway ("39% of QC learners struggle to read"), not the topic ("The Problem").

- **Deck for:** [competition / investor / partner / customer]
- **Time limit:** [N minutes] — rehearse to [N−1]
- **Date:** [YYYY-MM-DD]

## Slide-by-slide structure

- [ ] **1. Title** — product name, one-line description a stranger understands, team name, contact
- [ ] **2. Problem** — the pain, sized and sourced (pull from market research §5). Make it human: one real person's story beats three statistics
- [ ] **3. Solution** — what it is and the "aha" in one sentence; screenshot or live demo beats feature lists
- [ ] **4. Why now** — what changed that makes this possible/urgent today
- [ ] **5. Market size** — TAM/SAM/SOM with the math visible (from market research §2)
- [ ] **6. Product / demo** — 2-3 core features max, each stated as user outcome, not tech spec
- [ ] **7. Competition** — honest landscape incl. status quo; your wedge highlighted (from competitor analysis §3)
- [ ] **8. Business model** — who pays, how much, unit economics headline (from pricing worksheet §4)
- [ ] **9. Traction / validation** — pilots, interviews, LOIs, prototype progress. Any real usage > all projections
- [ ] **10. Team** — why *you* win this market: relevant skills, achievements, unfair access
- [ ] **11. Roadmap / ask** — what's next and exactly what you're asking for (funding amount, pilot access, mentorship)
- [ ] **12. Closing** — the one line you want remembered + contact info

## Quality pass

- [ ] Every number on every slide has a source you can cite when asked
- [ ] Numbers are consistent across deck, business plan, and BRD (one source of truth)
- [ ] Each slide headline is a full-sentence takeaway; the deck makes sense reading headlines alone
- [ ] ≤ 30 words per slide body; no paragraph anywhere
- [ ] Readable from the back of a room: big type, high contrast, one chart max per slide
- [ ] Jargon-free — a smart 15-year-old could follow it
- [ ] Demo has a fallback (video recording) in case live fails

## Delivery prep

- [ ] Rehearsed out loud [3+] times, at least once to someone who gives hard feedback
- [ ] Timed under the limit with buffer for setup
- [ ] Q&A prep: written answers for the 10 hardest questions ([unit economics, competition, defensibility, why you, what if DepEd says no…])
- [ ] Judges' scoring criteria mapped: each criterion clearly addressed by a specific slide ([impact → slides 2,5] [innovation → 3,7] [feasibility → 8,9] [team → 10])
- [ ] Backup: deck as PDF on two devices + USB; works without internet

## After the pitch

- [ ] Log every question asked — repeated questions mean a slide is failing
- [ ] Update the deck within 48h while feedback is fresh
