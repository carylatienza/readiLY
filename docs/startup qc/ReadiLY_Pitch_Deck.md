# ReadiLY [cite: 1]
ReadiLY is an AI-powered oral reading intelligence system that transforms a 3-minute reading activity into real-time diagnosis, prediction, and intervention. [cite: 1]

## Executive Summary [cite: 1]
* **THE CONCEPT:** ReadiLY is an AI-powered literacy intelligence system that analyzes students' oral reading in real time to diagnose learning gaps and enable immediate, data-driven intervention. [cite: 1]
* **5-YEAR VISION:** To scale ReadiLY as a standard literacy assessment system across public schools, enabling real-time diagnosis, data-driven teaching, and city-wide literacy intelligence by 2031. [cite: 1]
* **THE GAP:** Current assessments are manual, slow, and non-diagnostic. Teachers receive scores but not explanations, creating a visibility gap in identifying why students struggle. [cite: 1]
* **IMPACT:** ReadiLY empowers students and educators by transforming reading into actionable intelligence—enabling faster learning, better teaching, and long-term workforce readiness. [cite: 1]

## The Issue & The Gap [cite: 1]
**Meet Alex:** 8 Year Old, Grade 2 Student. [cite: 1]

**The Issue**
Many students in Quezon City struggle with reading, yet teachers lack tools to assess and support them effectively. [cite: 1] Current systems are broken in three ways:
1. They provide scores but no diagnosis ("black box") [cite: 1]
2. Assessments are manual and time-consuming (15-20 minutes per student) [cite: 1]
3. There is no scalable system to monitor and intervene early [cite: 1]

This results in delayed intervention and persistent learning gaps. [cite: 1]

**The Gap**
Current solutions fail because they are either:
* **Assessment tools** → provide scores but no diagnosis [cite: 1]
* **Learning apps** → provide content but no real-time insight [cite: 1]

No system tells teachers why a student is struggling and what to do next. This creates a systemic visibility gap in classrooms. [cite: 1]

**Primary Research**
We observed and validated that:
* Teachers rely on manual or Excel-based tracking systems [cite: 1]
* Assessments like reading tests are still administered one-by-one [cite: 1]
* There is no real-time feedback mechanism [cite: 1]
* Teachers expressed difficulty in identifying specific learning gaps quickly and accurately. [cite: 1]

**Urgency**
This problem must be solved now because:
* Early literacy gaps compound over time [cite: 1]
* Students fall behind before intervention happens [cite: 1]
* Workforce readiness is affected at a foundational level [cite: 1]

If we don't fix reading early, we pay for it later—in education, in productivity, and in the economy. [cite: 1]

## Solution Features [cite: 1]
* **AI-Powered Oral Reading Assessment:** Captures and analyzes student reading in real time using a teacher's smartphone, replacing manual and time-consuming assessment. Outcome: Assess an entire class in minutes instead of hours. [cite: 1]
* **Real-Time Learning Gap Diagnosis:** Analyzes reading performance instantly to identify specific literacy gaps as students read. Outcome: Teachers move from guessing to knowing exactly what each student needs. [cite: 1]
* **Early Warning System:** Catch learning gaps before they grow. Gives users full control with Smart Silence, transparent eligibility checks, and real-time assistance for a calmer, safer app experience. Outcome: Enables precise, targeted intervention—not one-size-fits-all teaching. [cite: 1]
* **Teacher Intelligence Dashboard:** Provides a clear view of student performance, class trends, and key literacy gaps through a simple, actionable interface. Outcome: Teachers make faster, data-driven decisions with confidence. [cite: 1]

## Proof of Concept [cite: 1]
* **Prototype/MVP:** Developed a working prototype of ReadiLY's core system, focusing on the end-to-end reading assessment flow: Student reading input, Audio capture and processing, and Initial diagnostic output. Validates the core concept of real-time literacy analysis. [cite: 1]
* **Development Progress:** Actively developed throughout the competition timeline. Completed core system logic for reading capture, error detection structure, and diagnostic output flow. Demonstrates strong execution speed and technical feasibility. [cite: 1]
* **Initial Validation:** Conducted internal and simulated testing scenarios. Confirmed smooth input-to-output workflow, fast response time for analysis, and clear, interpretable outputs. Validates usability and system behavior in classroom-like conditions. [cite: 1]
* **Technical Feasibility:** Built using proven AI approaches (speech recognition + pattern analysis). Designed for mobile-first and offline-capable deployment. Scalable architecture for future model improvement. Technically viable for real-world implementation. [cite: 1]

## Market Analysis [cite: 1]
**Sizing**
The Philippines allocated P1.37 trillion to education in 2026, yet foundational literacy gaps persist across public schools (Department of Budget and Management, 2026). [cite: 1] In Quezon City, 39.71% of learners are classified as struggling readers, highlighting a significant need for scalable literacy intervention and diagnostic solutions (Quezon City CRLA Report, 2025-2026). [cite: 1]

**Demographics**
* **Who they are:** Primary users are public elementary school teachers (Grades 1-6) and their students in Quezon City, particularly in classrooms with high student-to-teacher ratios. [cite: 1]
* **Their Habits:** Teachers conduct manual, one-by-one reading assessments using basic tools (paper-based or spreadsheets). Assessments are periodic, not continuous, with limited access to real-time diagnostic feedback. [cite: 1]
* **The Pain:** Assessments take 15-20 minutes per student, making full-class evaluation impractical. Teachers receive scores without explanation, limiting intervention quality. Learning gaps are identified too late, when remediation is harder. [cite: 1]
* **The Cost of the Problem:** Lost instructional time, increased teacher workload and burnout, and long-term impact on student literacy and academic progression. [cite: 1]

**Go-To-Market**
* **The Partnership Strategy:** Collaborate with Quezon City Education Department (DepEd QC), school administrators, teachers, and local education stakeholders. This ensures institutional support, faster adoption, and integration into existing workflows. [cite: 1]
* **The Beachhead (Starting Point):** Launch pilot deployment in 1-3 public schools in Quezon City, focusing on classrooms with high literacy gaps to validate effectiveness in real teaching environments. [cite: 1]
* **The Adoption Strategy (The Hook):** Offer a free pilot program that delivers immediate value: real-time reading assessment, instant learning gap diagnosis, and reduced assessment time (from hours to minutes). Teachers experience impact on Day 1. [cite: 1]
* **Scaling Strategy:** Expand from pilot schools to city-wide deployment. Leverage LGU validation to scale to other NCR cities. Position ReadiLY as a standard literacy assessment system. [cite: 1]
