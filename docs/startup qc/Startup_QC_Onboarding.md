### What is a startup?
* **Steve Blank:** A startup searches for a repeatable, scalable business model.
* **Eric Ries:** Startups are temporary institutions aiming for sustainability, facing high uncertainty.
* **Paul Graham:** Startups are designed to grow fast.

#### Key Differentiation of a Startup versus an MSME
| | MSME | STARTUP |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Core Definition** | Operates on known, proven business models to fulfill existing market demand. | Focuses on unproven, highly innovative products, services, or business models. |
| **Role of Technology** | Used as a tool to improve efficiency (e.g., POS systems, basic websites). | Technology is the core product or delivery mechanism (e.g., software, apps). |
| **Growth Model** | Linear: Revenue grows in proportion to physical resources, labor, and capital. | Exponential: High upfront costs lead to rapid, massive scaling with minimal incremental costs. |
| **Primary Funding** | Personal savings, traditional bank loans, and family capital (Debt-driven). | Venture Capital (VC), Angel Investors, and equity crowdfunding (Equity-driven). |
| **Financial Priority** | Immediate profitability, steady cash flow, and financial sustainability. | Rapid market capture, user acquisition, and scaling (often running at a loss initially). |
| **Market Reach** | Local, provincial, or regional communities. | "Born global" - Built to scale across international borders from day one via digital platforms. |
| **Risk Profile** | Lower Risk: Operates in established industries with predictable outcomes. | High Risk: Operates in highly uncertain environments (roughly a 90% failure rate). |
| **Ultimate Goal / Exit** | Long-term operational longevity, financial stability, or family succession. | A defined "Exit" within 5-10 years via acquisition by a larger company or an IPO. |
| **PH Legal Framework** | RA 9501 (Magna Carta for MSMEs): Strictly classified by asset size (up to 100M max). | RA 11337 (Innovative Startup Act): Defined by innovation, scalability, and intellectual property. |

### What we look for in startups
1. **TEAM:** Passion, Original Founders Background, Investors vs. Entrepreneurs, Focus, Serial entrepreneur?, "It" factor, Early Hires.
2. **TECHNOLOGY:** Scalable, Horizontal vs Vertical, Batch vs Continuous, Market fit, Data is gold, Easy to understand, Economical, IP Assessment, IP Defensibility and strength, Competitive Advantage.
3. **BUSINESS:** Market, Real business vs Patent troll, Clear business model, Problem solving or Buzzword driven?, Burn rate, Accomplishments/Duration.
4. **TRACTION:** Customers, Sales Pipeline, Total Available Market, Which customers are they speaking to?, Early investors, POC's (Paid v. Unpaid), Relative Traction.

### Startup Evaluation 101
* **Sourcing (1-2 weeks)** -> **Initial Assessment (1-4 weeks)** -> **Due Diligence (2-8 weeks)** -> **Deal Negotiation (1-2 weeks)** -> **IC Approval (1-2 weeks)** -> **Legal Process (1-2 weeks)** -> **Money Transfer (1 week)**.
* *Startups can be dropped at any stage for various reasons (e.g., not raising, too early/late, not meeting requirements, lack of excitement, red flags).*
