## Problem
1. **Manual & Time-Intensive Assessments:** Teachers conduct oral reading assessments one student at a time, making it difficult to evaluate entire classrooms efficiently.
2. **Delayed Identification of Learning Gaps:** Students with reading difficulties are often identified only after they have fallen significantly behind, limiting opportunities for early intervention.
3. **Limited Diagnostic Intelligence:** Existing literacy assessments provide scores but lack AI-driven insights into fluency, decoding, error patterns, and personalized intervention strategies.

### Existing alternatives
1. Phil-IRI, CRLA, and other DepEd literacy assessment tools.
2. Manual teacher observations, printed assessment forms, and spreadsheet-based tracking.
3. Digital reading applications focused on practice rather than diagnostic assessment.

---

## Solution
1. **AI-Powered Oral Reading Assessment:** An offline-first mobile application that analyzes students' oral reading using Automatic Speech Recognition (ASR) and speech analytics.
2. **Reading Intelligence Dashboard:** Generates instant diagnostics, learning gap identification, and early warning indicators for teachers.
3. **Teacher Decision Support:** Provides actionable classroom insights that guide targeted reading interventions while integrating with existing literacy assessment workflows.

### Key Metrics
1. **Assessment Efficiency:** Reduction in oral reading assessment time (target: 80%, from approximately 15 minutes to 3 minutes per learner).
2. **Teacher Adoption:** Number of teachers and schools actively using ReadiLY during pilot implementation.
3. **Student Coverage:** Percentage of learners receiving AI-assisted diagnostic assessments.
4. **Intervention Impact:** Number of identified literacy gaps supported through targeted interventions.
